With a robust [b]defense[/b], mastery of [b]shield blocking[/b], and powerful tools for generating and maintaining aggro, the [b]Paladin[/b] ensures the safety of their team in the face of any threat.

Thanks to its versatility and powerful [b]defensive capabilities[/b], the [b]Paladin[/b] thrives in a wide variety of situations. With abilities like [b][color=#4db2ff]Majesty[/color][/b], which significantly enhances its [b]healing[/b] and group management, the [b]Paladin[/b] handles large groups of enemies and formidable bosses with ease. It is arguably the best tank for [b]Dynamis Divergence[/b] and any content featuring bosses that deal primarily physical damage, while remaining fully capable of mitigating magical damage when a [b]Rune Fencer[/b] is unavailable. Its strategic positioning and survival tools make it indispensable, even in the most challenging encounters.[/size][/div]

[size=20][b][div id="section-ce-loss-reduction"][color=#daa520]Optimizing CE Loss Reduction with Gear and Enmity+[/color][/b][/size][size=16][url=#section-top style="font-size:14px;"][color=#56a9f7]Back to top[/color][/url][div style="text-align:left; font-size:15px"][div align="center"]
[size=20][b][color=#daa520]Total CE Loss Cap[/color][/b][/size]
[color=#ffffff]In tanking mechanics, minimizing [color=#ffb380]Cumulative Enmity (CE)[/color] loss is crucial for maintaining aggro, especially in multi-target scenarios. This is achieved through a combination of [color=#ffb380]Enmity+[/color] stats, gear effects with [color=#ffb380]Reduces Enmity Loss[/color] properties, and [color=#39FF14]Foe Sirvente[/color]. The reduction works multiplicatively, allowing for significant mitigation when all factors are optimized.[/color]

[tr style="background-color:#333333;"]
[td colspan="3" style="color:#ffffff; text-align:center; padding:10px; vertical-align:middle;"][b]Maximum Total Reduction: [color=#ffa366]75%[/color][/b]  
This is achieved through [color=#ffb380]50% Enmity+[/color] and [color=#ffb380]50% Gear/Foe Sirvente[/color], applied multiplicatively:  
[b][color=#ffa366]Total Reduction = 1 - [(1 - 0.50) × (1 - 0.50)] = 75%[/color][/b].[/td][/tr]
[/table]
[size=18][b][color=#daa520]Simplified Calculation Example[/color][/b][/size]
[color=#ffffff]
If you have:
• [color=#ffb380]+100 Enmity[/color] ([color=#ffa366]50% reduction[/color])  
• [color=#ffb380]50% Gear + Foe Sirvente Reduction[/color]

The total CE loss reduction is:  
[b][color=#ffa366]Total Reduction = 1 - [(1 - 0.50) × (1 - 0.50)] = 75%[/color][/b].  
This means only [b][color=#ffa366]25%[/color][/b] of your CE is lost when taking damage.  
[/color]
[size=18][b][color=#daa520]Important Notes and Strategy[/color][/b][/size]
[color=#ffffff]
• Focus on survivability stats ([b][color=#ffb380]DT, PDT, MDT[/color][/b]) before maximizing CE loss reduction.  
• Use [b][color=#ffb380]Foe Sirvente[/color][/b] strategically in multi-target fights where CE loss is critical.  
• Balance your gear to avoid overcapping CE reduction.  
[/color]

[size=20][b][color=#daa520]Healing Enmity Mechanics[/color][/b][/size]

[size=14][b][color=#ffffff]Enmity Generated by Healing[/color][/b][color=#ffffff]
• [b][color=#ffa366]Cumulative Enmity (CE)[/color][/b] = HP Healed × [CE Modifier]
• [b][color=#ff8533]Volatile Enmity (VE)[/color][/b] = CE × 6
• For level 99+ targets, modifier is fixed at [b]0.231[/b]
• CE and VE are divided by number of enemies that have the healer on their hate list and are within enmity range
• Enmity+ bonus is applied after mob division
• Base CE can be 0 with enmity- gear or Tranquil Heart, otherwise minimum 1 CE[/color][/size]

[size=22][b][color=#daa520]General Concept: Balanced Mitigation[/color][/b][/size][table style="width:95%; margin:0 auto;"][tr][td style="text-align:left; padding-left:20px; color:#ffffff;"][size=15 style="line-height:1.8;"]
Magic resistance requires adapting your equipment to the context: a [b][color=#ffd77c]generalist Idle set[/color][/b] for everyday use and a [b][color=#ffd77c]specialized Idle MDT set[/color][/b] for magic-heavy encounters.

[b][color=#ffd77c]Generalist Idle Set:[/color][/b]
• A generalist set offers balanced mitigation but is limited for magic resistance, especially against high-level enemies (130+) where MEva will generally be insufficient to resist frequently due to the high magic accuracy (macc) of these mobs.
• In such situations, [b]Magic Defense Bonus (MDB)[/b] becomes more relevant to reduce magic damage.
• This type of set is still suitable for mixed content like Dynamis Divergence, where both physical and magical damage mitigation is necessary, using, for example, the [color=#daa520]Chevalier’s Set + Adamantite Armor[/color].

[b][color=#ffd77c]Idle MDT Set:[/color][/b]
• Idle MDT sets are crucial for magic-heavy content or for specific bosses in events like Sortie or Odyssey Sheol Gaol.
• They allow:

1. Reaching the Magic Damage Taken (MDT) cap of 87.5% with [color=#daa520]Aegis[/color].
2. Increasing MEva and elemental resistances to unlock full resistances (1/8 damage).
3. Maximizing MDB to further reduce magic damage.
   • This set is essential against enemies specifically focused on magic, where a generalist set would be insufficient.

[b][color=#ffd77c]Magic Evasion (MEva):[/color][/b]
• Increases the chances of partially or fully resisting magical attacks.
• Full resistance (1/8 damage) is only possible if you have positive elemental resistance.
• Effective against enemies with moderate magic accuracy (levels 120-125), but less effective against high-level enemies (130+).

[b][color=#ffd77c]Magic Defense Bonus (MDB):[/color][/b]
• Directly reduces magic damage, essential in MDT sets for enemies with high magic accuracy (level 130+).

[b][color=#ffd77c]Elemental Resistances:[/color][/b]
• Ensure positive resistances to unlock full resistances (1/8 damage).
• Negative resistances prevent full resistance and limit damage reduction to a minimum of 50%.
[/size][/td][/tr][/table]

[size=22][b][color=#daa520]Practical Strategies[/color][/b][/size][table style="width:95%; margin:0 auto;"][tr][td style="text-align:left; padding-left:20px; color:#ffffff;"][size=15 style="line-height:1.8;"]
[b][color=#ffd77c]1. Balancing MEva and MDT:[/color][/b]
• For generalist sets, prioritize equipment with high MEva like [color=#daa520]Sakpata’s Set[/color] against enemies of levels 120-125.
• Against enemies with high magic accuracy (level 130+), switch to MDB-focused sets such as [color=#daa520]Chevalier’s Set + Adamantite Armor[/color] for better magic damage reduction.
• For purely magical content, opt for an Idle MDT set with the MDT cap (87.5%) reached using [color=#daa520]Aegis[/color], then maximize MEva and elemental resistances.

[b][color=#ffd77c]2. Using Buffs:[/color][/b]
• [b]Shell:[/b] Reduces magic damage taken (MDT) depending on the spell level:

- [b]Shell IV:[/b] -67/256 (-26%)
- [b]Shell V:[/b] -75/256 (-29%)
  • Always use [b]Phalanx[/b] to further reduce residual magic damage.

[b][color=#ffd77c]3. Resistance Stability:[/color][/b]
• Equip items like the [color=#daa520]Carrier’s Sash[/color] (+15 elemental resistances) to maintain positive resistances in all circumstances.
• Use [b]Barspells[/b] to address specific elemental weaknesses during encounters.

[b][color=#ffd77c][u]Solution:[/u][/color][/b]
• Adjust the HP of your sets to align with the one with the lowest HP (often Fast Cast).  
• Limit the HP gap to around [b][color=#80ff80]200HP[/color] [color=#ffd77c]maximum[/color][/b].

[b][color=#ffd77c][u]Adjusted Example:[/u][/color][/b]

• [color=#66ccff]Enmity Set:[/color] [color=#80ff80]2900 HP[/color]

Result: Transitions become smooth without excessive variations, avoiding confusion and unnecessary healing.
