[size=25][b][color=#ffd700]Introduction to the Paladin Role[/color][/b][/size]

[div style="margin:25px auto; background-color:#444444; width:95%; margin-top:25px; padding:25px; border-radius:10px; border:3px solid #000000; box-shadow:0 0 15px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.7);"][size=15 style="line-height:1.8; color:#ffffff;"]
[div style="width:95%"]The Paladin stands at the forefront of battle, shielding allies by absorbing attacks and keeping enemies focused on them. With a robust defense, mastery of shield blocking, and powerful tools for generating and maintaining aggro, the Paladin ensures the safety of their team in the face of any threat.

Thanks to its versatility and powerful defensive capabilities, the Paladin thrives in a wide variety of situations. With abilities like [b][color=#ffdf80]Majesty[/color][/b], which significantly enhances its healing and group management, the Paladin handles large groups of enemies and formidable bosses with ease. It is arguably the best tank for Dynamis Divergence and any content featuring bosses that deal primarily physical damage, while remaining fully capable of mitigating magical damage when a [b][color=#ffdf80]Rune Fencer[/color][/b] is unavailable. Its strategic positioning and survival tools make it indispensable, even in the most challenging encounters.[/div] [/size]
[/div]

[div style="width:95%"][size=25][b][color=#ffd700]Gameplay Basics[/color][/b][/size]
[size=15 style="text-align:left; color:#ffffff;"]
As a Paladin, your main responsibility is to maintain aggro and ensure enemies stay focused on you, keeping your allies safe. [size=25][b][color=#ffd700]Gear Optimization and Transition Management[/color][/b][/size]

[size=22][b][color=#ffa500]General Concept: HP Stability[/color][/b][/size]
[table style="width:95%; margin:0 auto;"]
[tr][td style="text-align:left; padding-left:20px; color:#ffffff;"]
[size=15 style="line-height:1.8;"]
During combat, every transition between gear sets (e.g., [color=#ffdf80]Idle → Fast Cast → Enmity[/color]) can cause HP fluctuations. These fluctuations appear as missing HP, confusing healers and leading to unnecessary healing cycles.

[b][color=#ffdf80]Problem:[/color][/b]  
If your Idle set has [color=#80ff80]3200 HP[/color] and your Fast Cast set has [color=#80ff80]2800 HP[/color], each transition brings your HP down to [color=#80ff80]2800[/color], even if your max HP returns to [color=#80ff80]3200[/color]. This gives the illusion that you’ve "lost" [color=#ff4d4d]400 HP[/color], prompting healers to heal unnecessarily.

[b][color=#ffdf80]Solution:[/color][/b]  
• Limit HP gaps between your sets to maintain stability.  
• Adjust your overall HP to be close to the set with the lowest HP (often Fast Cast).  
• Use GearSwap to structure transitions with smart priorities. [/size][/td][/tr][/table]
[/div]

• [b][color=#66ccff]Idle Set:[/color][/b] [color=#80ff80]3200 HP[/color]
• [b][color=#66ccff]Fast Cast Set:[/color][/b] [color=#80ff80]2800 HP[/color]

Each transition drops your HP to 2800, even if your max HP returns to 3200, creating apparent "missing HP" (2800/3200). This leads to unnecessary healing and wasted resources.

[b][color=#ffdf80][u]Solution:[/u][/color][/b]
• Adjust the HP of your sets to align with the one with the lowest HP (often Fast Cast).  
• Limit the HP gap to around [b][color=#80ff80]200HP[/color] [color=#ffdf80]maximum[/color][/b].

[b][color=#ffdf80][u]Adjusted Example:[/u][/color][/b]
• [color=#66ccff]Idle Set:[/color] [color=#80ff80]2900 HP[/color]
• [color=#66ccff]Fast Cast Set:[/color] [color=#80ff80]2800 HP[/color]
• [color=#66ccff]Enmity Set:[/color] [color=#80ff80]2900 HP[/color]

Result: Transitions become smooth without excessive variations, avoiding confusion and unnecessary healing.
[/size][/td][/tr][/table]
[/div]

[size=22][b][color=#ffa500]Conclusion: Adapt HP and Prioritize Intelligently[/color][/b][/size]
[table style="width:95%; margin:0 auto;"]
[tr][td style="text-align:left; padding-left:20px; color:#ffffff;"]
[size=15 style="line-height:1.8;"]

1. [b][color=#ffdf80]With GearSwap:[/color][/b] Use priorities to manage the equip order and avoid visible HP loss.
2. [b][color=#ffdf80]Without GearSwap:[/color][/b] Adjust your sets to limit HP differences and maintain maximum stability.
3. Align your overall HP to the set with the lowest HP (e.g., Fast Cast) to minimize variations.
4. Structure your transitions to avoid unnecessary healing cycles caused by apparent HP gaps.

Whether you use GearSwap or manual commands, these principles ensure optimized HP management and stable transitions during combat.[/size][/td][/tr][/table]
